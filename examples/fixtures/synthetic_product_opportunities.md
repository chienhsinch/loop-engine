# Synthetic AI product opportunity brief

This fixture is fictional. Its statements are candidate hypotheses for a software demonstration, not evidence of real market demand.

## Candidate A: Physical-therapy follow-up assistant

- Proposed user: small outpatient physical-therapy clinics.
- Problem hypothesis: staff spend time turning visit notes into patient-friendly exercise reminders and follow-up messages.
- Synthetic signal: three fictional clinic profiles describe follow-up consistency as an operational concern.
- Possible advantage: narrow workflow, repeated use, and artifacts that can be reviewed before sending.
- Key risks: sensitive health information, clinical-safety boundaries, integration burden, and uncertain willingness to pay.

## Candidate B: Manufacturing RFP comparison assistant

- Proposed user: sales and operations teams at mid-market component manufacturers.
- Problem hypothesis: teams manually compare long customer request-for-proposal documents against internal capability sheets.
- Synthetic signal: two fictional team profiles report slow handoffs and repeated spreadsheet-based comparison.
- Possible advantage: a bounded document-analysis workflow with an inspectable gap report.
- Key risks: source-document variability, confidential data, accuracy requirements, and unclear buying ownership.

## Candidate C: Course-feedback synthesis assistant

- Proposed user: instructors and teaching teams for large university courses.
- Problem hypothesis: free-text feedback is time-consuming to organize into actionable themes.
- Synthetic signal: four fictional course profiles contain recurring complaints about delayed feedback analysis.
- Possible advantage: simple batch analysis and a human-reviewable report.
- Key risks: seasonal usage, limited budgets, privacy expectations, and many existing survey tools.

## Demonstration boundary

The first objective should compare or investigate these candidates using only this brief. Any selected candidate remains a hypothesis. A later objective may design a real evidence-producing validation step, but this fixture cannot establish demand or willingness to pay.
